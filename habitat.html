Natural Habitat

Axolotls are native to the ancient lake system of Xochimilco near Mexico City. This network of canals and wetlands is the only remaining habitat of these unique creatures, maintained at a constant temperature of 20-24°C (68-72°F) year-round. Source: Wikipedia

Environmental Threats

Urban expansion in Mexico City has led to severe habitat fragmentation. Pollution from agricultural runoff and invasive species like tilapia and carp have dramatically altered the ecosystem, pushing axolotls to the brink of extinction. Source: IUCN Red List

Conservation efforts focus on creating chinampa refuges - traditional agricultural islands that provide shaded, clean water habitats. These artificial shelters help maintain population clusters while restoration projects continue. Source: TecScience
