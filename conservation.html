Conservation Status

Listed as Critically Endangered by IUCN, wild populations have declined by 99.9% since 2000. Recent surveys estimate fewer than 1,000 individuals remain in the wild. Source: IUCN

The Mexican government launched "Refugio Chinampa" in 2017, establishing protected zones in Xochimilco. These conservation areas use filters to maintain water quality and exclude invasive species. Source: TecScience

Captive Breeding

International breeding programs maintain genetic diversity. The Ambystoma Genetic Stock Center houses over 2,000 axolotls for research and conservation purposes. Source: Ambystoma Genetic Stock Center

Public awareness campaigns educate local communities about sustainable practices. "Adopt an Axolotl" programs fund habitat restoration while engaging citizens in conservation efforts. Source: NPR

"Every axolotl saved represents hope for Mexico's unique aquatic heritage."
