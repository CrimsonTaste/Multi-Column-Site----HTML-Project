Unique Biology

Axolotls exhibit paedomorphism, retaining juvenile characteristics into adulthood. Their genome is 10 times larger than human DNA, containing about 32 billion base pairs - the largest genome ever sequenced. Source: MPG

  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in captivity
  • Size: 15-45 cm (6-18 inches)
  • Diet: Carnivorous (worms, insects, small fish)
  • Skin: Permeable for respiration
  • Gills: Three pairs of external gills

Regeneration

Axolotls can regenerate complete limbs in 40-50 days. This process involves dedifferentiation of cells into stem cells, which then rebuild complex structures including bones, muscles, and nerves. Source: Axolotl Nerd

Their immune system plays a crucial role in regeneration. Macrophages (immune cells) are essential for proper regeneration - without them, axolotls form scar tissue instead of regenerating. Source: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences

"In terms of regeneration, axolotls are the superheroes of the vertebrate world."
